What is the LUNA Score?

LUNA (Lineup Underdog Normalized Activation) v2.0 is an uncompromising, per-possession metric designed to measure a player's true offensive impact strictly against elite NBA defenses.

LUNAv2.0 = [PCscaled × Log(Poss)] × (1 + ΔTSEra)2.5

The v2.0 Methodology

  • The Elite Filter: The engine isolates performances solely against top-tier teams, filtering out stats padded against lottery-bound rosters.
  • Logarithmic Volume Scaling: It strips away the inflation of heavy playing time while using a logarithmic curve to reward true offensive anchors and stabilize the volatile spikes of low-minute bench players.
  • Era-Adjusted Multiplier: It calculates a player's True Shooting differential against the actual league average of that specific season, then applies a smoothed 2.5 exponent. This mathematically rewards hyper-efficiency while strictly penalizing forced, low-quality shots across any era of basketball.

Why is it Valuable?

In modern basketball, raw box-score totals can be a statistical trap. High-usage players can mask terrible efficiency with sheer volume, while elite role players are frequently undervalued because they take fewer shots. LUNA operates as a truth machine for front offices:

  • Exposes "Empty Calories": It identifies volume shooters who actively hurt their team by absorbing possessions inefficiently under playoff-level defensive pressure.
  • Identifies Market Inefficiencies: It highlights disciplined "analytics darlings" who maximize every sliver of opportunity, proving their game can scale in high-stakes environments.
