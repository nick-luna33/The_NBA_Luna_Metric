What is LUNA Truly Measuring?

LUNA (Lineup Underdog Normalized Activation) v3.5 is an uncompromising analytics engine designed to measure a player's scalable offensive equity under pressure. It strips away the noise of raw box scores to answer one specific question: When the defense is elite, the game is competitive, and the ball is in your hands, how much true value do you generate for your team per possession?

LUNAv3.5 = [PCscaled × Log(Poss)] × (1 + ΔTCEra)2.5

The Equation Broken Down

  • Standardized Creation (PCscaled): Takes a player's total points scored plus their exact points generated from assists, scaled to a baseline of 20 possessions. This levels the playing field between high-minute starters and low-minute role players.
  • The Workload Tax (Log(Poss)): Maintaining efficiency is exponentially harder at high volume. This logarithmic curve stabilizes the math by penalizing small sample sizes and rewarding superstars who shoulder massive offensive loads without collapsing.
  • Era-Adjusted True Creation (ΔTCEra): True Creation % improves upon True Shooting by factoring in assists while violently punishing turnovers. The engine dynamically subtracts the specific era's league-average TC% from the player's TC%, ensuring players in the gritty 2000s are judged fairly against players in the modern pace-and-space era.
  • The Smoothed Exponent (2.5): This multiplier acts as a truth serum. It mathematically obliterates the scores of high-volume, turnover-prone chuckers, while exponentially rewarding hyper-efficient play. The 2.5 curve ensures primary scoring options aren't unfairly punished for drawing double-teams.

The Environmental Filters

LUNA completely ignores games where stats are artificially padded. A game is only passed into the engine if it survives two checks:

  • The True Defense Filter: The opponent must have an average Points Allowed rating that is strictly better than the league average up to that point in the season.
  • The "Fake Comeback" Garbage Time Filter: The engine voids 4th quarter garbage-time padding by throwing out any game where a team was down by 20+ points at the end of the 3rd quarter AND ended up losing by 20+ points.
